üîé Processing overview

Goal: replace water with a solid medium (paraffin/resin) while preserving structure, targets, and dimensions.

  1. Dehydration ‚Äî graded alcohols remove free/bound water.
  2. Clearing ‚Äî solvent miscible with alcohol + paraffin displaces alcohol.
  3. Infiltration ‚Äî molten paraffin (or resin) replaces clearing solvent.

Control levers: ‚â§3‚Äì5 mm thickness, dwell time, bath temperature (avoid overheating), vacuum/pressure, agitation.

ü¶¥ Decalcification ‚Äî method, endpoint, downstream impact

When: bone, teeth, calcified vessels, some marrow cores.

Methods
  • EDTA pH ~7‚Äì7.4 ‚Äî best morphology/antigen/NA; slower.
  • Acids ‚Äî formic (moderate speed/preservation), HCl/Nitric (fastest; most damaging).
  • Microwave-assisted ‚Äî accelerates; strict control needed.
  • Ion-exchange resins ‚Äî can speed EDTA.
Endpoint monitoring
  • Chemical (ammonium oxalate test on spent solution).
  • Radiography (dense cores/teeth).
  • Mechanical (least reliable).
  • Timed charts (guides only; validate locally).
Downstream impacts
  • Acids ‚Üí weaker hematoxylin, reduced silver & IHC signals; risk false negatives.
  • EDTA ‚Üí slower TAT but superior morphology/antigen/NA preservation.
EDTA working tip

Maintain pH 7‚Äì7.4, agitate, refresh; verify endpoint.

Chemical endpoint blurb

Ammonium oxalate check; beware acid overuse artifacts.

üßæ QC, reagent management & safety

  • Rotation: last bath ‚Üí first; discard dirtiest; track by cassettes processed + quick checks (SG for alcohol; cloudiness for xylene).
  • Logs: station times/temps, vacuum, alarms, reagent changes, lot numbers.
  • Controls: include a processing control (e.g., liver/uterus) when schedules or reagents change.
  • Safety: xylene/toluene (flammable, neurotoxic) ‚Üí ventilation/PPE; limonene (sensitizer); hot paraffin burns; waste segregation.

üßØ Troubleshooting decision trees

  • Cloudy xylene ‚Üí water carryover. Fix: refresh 100% alcohols & xylene; check dwell & cassette load.
  • Mushy centers / edge-only infiltration ‚Üí poor paraffin infiltration. Fix: extend paraffin (add bath), raise MP slightly, apply vacuum in paraffin.
  • Brittle, shattery tissue ‚Üí over-dehydration or prolonged xylene. Fix: shorten absolute alcohol; reduce clearing dwell/temperature; try lower MP paraffin.
  • Retic/silver weak post-decalc ‚Üí acid decalc. Fix: document; prefer EDTA for IHC/ISH/silver cases.
  • Xylene odor in alcohols / eosin leach ‚Üí carryover contamination. Fix: enforce rotation & load limits; add intermediate alcohol if needed.
  • Tissue floats out at sectioning ‚Üí paraffin contamination or poor infiltration. Fix: reprocess; change paraffin baths; verify clearing completeness.

üßæ Summary & common pitfalls

  • Tune dwell, temperature, vacuum, and load to tissue type.
  • Watch carryover; rotate reagents; confirm endpoints.
  • Use EDTA for IHC/ISH-critical decalc; acid only with documented trade-offs.
  • Document QC and corrective actions.

Easy to get wrong

Show pitfalls

  • Ignoring cloudiness in xylene (water carryover).
  • Acid decalc before IHC/ISH without documenting risks.
