Fixation — Free Preview

Purpose of fixation, common fixatives, parameters (pH, temp, time, volume ratio), artifacts, and QC.

Overview

  • Objective: Stabilize tissue by cross-linking or precipitation to preserve morphology and antigenicity.
  • 10:1 fixative:tissue volume is a common rule; ensure adequate penetration and time.
  • NBF (10%) is standard; alternatives include alcoholic fixatives, Bouin’s (picric), Zenker’s (mercuric), and glutaraldehyde for EM.
  • Artifacts: autolysis, over-fixation, formalin pigment, shrinkage/swelling.
  • QC: pH monitoring, fixation time logging, labeling, and appropriate controls.

Learning objectives

  • Choose an appropriate fixative for a given tissue and downstream test.
  • State recommended fixation times and ratios for routine specimens.
  • Recognize common fixation artifacts and propose corrective actions.
